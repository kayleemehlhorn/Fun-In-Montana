Small Village, Big Adventure

Located in the Flathead Valley, nesetled within Montana's Rocky Mountains, Bigfork's 2020 census population was 5,118. In 2021 the median household income was $75,125, which is less than Whitefish but more than Helena. Living in Bigfork offers residents a suburban/rural mix feel. It is a great place for retirees!

Bigfork may be small, but there are quite a few things to enjoy here. During Memorial Day weekend, Bigfork's whitewater festival is going on. A must see event if you are in town during that time. There is also a theatre in town that puts on plays all summer long, which they call "Broadway in the Rockies." Bigfork also has quite the food scene, with amazing sushi, breweries, and best of all their breakfast joints.

Outside of town you will find lots of outdoor activities to keep you moving all year long. Flathead lake is a big lake that offers prime boating and fishing. Mt. Aneas is a summit hike 6 miles roundtrip that offers great views down into the valley, and you will most likely encounter some mountain goats along the way. And Blacktail ski resort offers lift access skiing/snowboarding to get your wintertime adreniline on. If you aren't into going downhill, there is also a plethora of nordic ski tracks to enjoy (especially with a furry friend).
