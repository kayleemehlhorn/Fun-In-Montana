Overview of the State of Montana

Montana is a state located in the Western region of the United States. It is a state that is low in population and rich in nature and animals.

Montana became the 41st state of the United States on November 8, 1889, with its capital being Helena.

A state known for its treasure and land, outdoor enthusiasts sure know where to go to get their kicks.

Economy

Montana's economy is based on agriculture, mining, forestry, and tourism.

Famous Landmarks

  • Going-to-the-Sun Road
  • Little Bighorn Battlefield National Monument
  • Geysers and hot springs (Yellowstone National Park)

Additional Information

  • State Bird:
    Western Meadowlark
  • State Flower:
    Bitteroot
  • State Motto:
    "Oro y Plata"
    (which is Spanish for gold and silver)
